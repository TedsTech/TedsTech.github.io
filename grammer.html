Independent Clause

An independent clause is a clause that can stand on its own, by itself. It does not need to be joined to any other clauses, because it contains all the information necessary to be a complete sentences.

  1. Independent clause. Independent clause.
    I love chocolate chip cookies. Michel loves Oreos.
  2. Independent clause, coordinating conjunction + independent clause.
    I love chocolate chip cookies, and Michel loves Oreos.
  3. Independent clause; independent clause.
    I love chocolate chip cookies; Michel loves Oreos.

Dependent Clause

A dependent clause is a group of words that also contains a subject and a verb, but it is not a complete thought.

  1. Because I woke up late. (what happened?)
  2. When we arrived at the beach. (what occurred?)
  3. If Kim does not pay his phone bill. (what will happen?)
